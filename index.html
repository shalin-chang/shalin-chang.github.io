My Year in Albums: 2026

Below are some albums I've been listening to this year, some that I've discovered recently and some I've been listening to but only recently understood them better.

When Broken is Easily Fixed - Silverstein (2004)

Favorite song: Bleeds No More

Listened to this while shoveling my driveway. Also love Red Light Pledge.


A City by the Light Divided - Thursday (2006)

Favorite song: Sugar in the Sacrament

I had the CD in my car in February and would listen to it a lot when driving off campus, taking my friends to local shows at Prescott Tavern and then driving them to Taco Bell where I'd watch as they waited an hour for their horrid blue drinks and deep-fried cinnamon sweets. I don't have a good memory of the rest of the album past the first few songs but I want to learn them on bass.


Full Collapse - Thursday (2001)

Favorite song: Autobiography of a Nation


GUTS - Olivia Rodrigo (2023)

Favorite song: pretty isn't pretty

I listened to this for the first time all the way through at the beginning of freshman year, while assembling puzzles on the second floor of the library. I also listened to it a fair amount around March this year while driving back and forth from home for spring break.


Moments Before The Wind - Free Throw (2026)


I Have No Mouth - I Have No Mouth (2025)

Favorite song: Bunny

I have a limited tolerance for listening to hardcore and screamo due to the fact that they've become so associated with poorly ventilated local shows choked in weed fumes and flailing necrophiliacs, to the point that listening to Connor Fisher's demos feels like inhaling secondhand smoke. It was a wonderful experience helping run sound with him at I Have No Mouth's last show at Prescott. Their set and their eponymous EP were both the perfect length for me; any longer and I'd get emphysema. Levi's guitar layers are really nice, the bass is thick and shoutout the drummer for being both awesome and nice to work with. Screams are incredible.


Permanence - No Devotion


Electric Ladyland - Jimi Hendrix


Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band - The Beatles

Favorite song: A Day In The Life

When I was younger, this album held a sort of forbidden mystique for me. Not because I wanted to take L.S.D. but was forbidden from doing so as a first grader, but because for many years we couldn't actually find the disc. I'd look at the artwork and the tracklist of the empty jewel case but I wouldn't be able to listen to the songs, despite knowing all of them. Later it turned out the CD was in my mom's car the whole time. Being a Paul meatrider, this album plus Abbey Road are very special for me.


Abbey Road - The Beatles

Favorite song: Oh! Darling

Like Sgt. Pepper, I've been listening to this album for almost my entire life. I somehow never picked up on the fact that the second half of the album is a medley of snippets until Geoff Emerick's Here, There and Everywhere pointed it out. I always assumed I was just mixing up parts of the songs in my head and thought "You Never Give Me Your Money" and "Carry That Weight" were the same song, which in my defense is a completely valid assumption. For that reason, I'm treating the medley as one epic mega-song, because I don't think any one snippet stands out well on its own, except "Because" for obvious reasons. Somehow I doubt anyone's favorite Beatles song is the minute-long Mean Mr. Mustard. Both sides (on vinyl/cassette) of the album are great too, not just the venerated second half: I love "Oh! Darling" and the proto-doom "I Want You". Fantastic album. Also happy for Mr. Starkey on getting in his song about the Grecian octopus.
